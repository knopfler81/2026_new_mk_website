Review

Sigh... I have no picture of that meeting. I didn’t dare ask the Man — MK was with his family.

I landed at BHX airport on Saturday, June 2nd, 2007. Phil and Pongsi, friends from Switzerland, were waiting for me there. It was our first meeting. We rented a car and travelled to Wales, destination Hay-on-Wye, to see our guitar hero.

The first event we attended was Kitty Aldridge, Mark’s wife, talking about her new book Cryers Hill, together with John Walsh. Mark was in the audience too, quite close to us. There I also met other fans like Val and her husband, and Martina.

After that interview, I met Mark outside — but only for ten seconds. Just enough time to say hi, happy to meet you, and shake hands. Always a pleasure to see him in person.

Sunday, June 3rd, was D-Day for Mark’s events — the real reason why we were there.

I met Matt, who had got me a ticket for the Hay-on-Sky broadcast. So I had the luck to attend the recording where Mark was interviewed about his career and played a new song, Secondary Waltz. Some authors were invited too. The broadcast was shown on British TV — funny to see my friends and me on screen, looking very serious.

After that, we went to another Mark Knopfler interview, this time in a bigger venue — I heard around 1,200 seats.

Mark was interviewed by Matthew Evans. He spoke about his songbook and played a few tunes with Guy Fletcher. Guy played everything — guitar, bass, keyboards.

Here is the set list:

Donegan’s Gone
Secondary Waltz
Rüdiger
Romeo and Juliet
Sailing to Philadelphia
Going Home
Song for Sonny Liston

Mark was very cool, pretty funny, and seemed in very good shape.

Monday the 4th, I came back to France. It was quite a long trip for such a tiny weekend, but my heart was full of memories.

Memory notes

  • Age: 26
  • With: Phil, Pongsi, Matt and other fans
  • Feeling: Excited, lucky and fulfilled
  • Highlight: Attending the Hay-on-Sky recording and hearing Secondary Waltz
  • Strongest memory: Seeing Mark in such an intimate setting and spending a whole weekend around his events in Hay-on-Wye
