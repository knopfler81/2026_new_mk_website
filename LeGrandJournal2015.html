25th March 2015

Review

First of all, a few of us made a naughty little joke: “MK wasn’t there, the broadcast is postponed to Monday, we don’t know why… we are so disappointed…” I’ll explain later…

We were asked to arrive at 4:00pm at the venue, and then… a lot of waiting. There were tons of teenagers on school trips, most of them had no idea who they were about to see.

We had to sign image rights papers, give our passports, leave our bags and jackets in a dressing room, and got a small numbered ticket like in a theatre. Then we waited again, almost one hour, in a waiting room.

When we finally entered the set, we were assigned seats. I tried to get closer to the stage… but no chance.

Before the show started, a floor manager explained how everything would go: first the “fun” music part, then the serious part about politics and current events. It was interesting to see how a TV show really works behind the scenes.

We had a break between Mark’s part and the political segment, with sodas and cake.

Just before the recording, we were told that Mark’s part would not be aired the same evening but the following Monday. Since all phones were forbidden (and even checked with detectors), we had no way to warn our friends. So we imagined them waiting at home… and that’s how our little joke started.

Mark was there, in very good shape, with Guy, Ianto, Dave Bronze and Robbie McIntosh (Paul Crockford and Glen Stagger too).

During the interview, it was quite hard to hear from the audience. The sound was low, and the French translation covered Mark’s voice, so it was a bit messy.

He talked about his guitar playing, describing how he holds the neck “like a plumber holds a pipe”… as he often says. He also mentioned being self-taught and praised his band as real musicians, as always very humble about himself.

There was also an interview with Léa Seydoux, the future James Bond girl. The presenter asked Mark if he would write a James Bond soundtrack — he said no, that film music is not really his thing.

Then Mark moved to the stage, just on my right — I was about five meters away. The view was great, and this time the sound was much better.

He played:

Beryl

There were actually two takes because of a technical issue that no one really noticed… but they preferred to do it again for the broadcast.

Off camera, Mark and Antoine de Caunes shared a big hug and took a picture together — they really looked like good old friends.

We were not allowed to approach Mark, but I managed to say hello to Guy and Glen from my seat. They are always so kind, always answering with a smile or a wave.

I thought they would play another song for the website, but when I saw the band leaving and the crew packing everything, I understood it was over. No extra song.

Mark left quickly, and we were asked to leave as well. Just for fun, with a big smile, I asked a security guy if I could go backstage to kiss Mark… “NO YOU CAN’T”. Well… I tried.

It was a very short moment, but a very good one. Always great to meet fans again.

We left the set around 8:30pm and had to queue again to get back our bags, jackets and passports. It was around 9:00pm when we finally left.

Memory notes

  • Age: 34
  • With: MK fans
  • Feeling: Curious, amused and happy
  • Highlight: Seeing Mark perform live on a TV set from just a few meters away
  • Strongest memory: The backstage atmosphere of a TV show
