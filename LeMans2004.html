Review

Friday morning, I searched for the place where the Le Mans racing cars get checked before the race. After several hours, I finally found the location and went straight to the paddocks — especially the one where Mark kept his car.

Once inside, my eyes were wide open, looking for the 1931 Invicta veteran racing car. I spent even more time searching before finally coming across a garage where some men were working on a car. Among them, I saw a man wearing a cap and dark glasses, signing something. I immediately knew — it was him.

The voice confirmed it. I approached, a little nervous, and quietly said:
Hello Mark!
Hello!
May I have an autograph please?

I gave him the cover of The Ragpicker’s Dream. While signing it, I told him it was so nice to see him. He replied that it was nice to see me too. That moment felt unreal.

Before leaving, I asked if I could give him a kiss — and he said yes. So I did. Two kisses. I simply couldn’t resist the moment.

I walked away, happy and a bit overwhelmed. I didn’t dare ask more — he was busy, and I was too emotional to even think clearly. Only later did I realize I had forgotten to ask for a picture.

I waited a while before trying again. At some point, I even joked to myself, asking a little ladybird to help me find him again. And somehow… I did.

This time, I asked for a photo. As always, he kindly accepted. One of the mechanics took the picture — twice, because the first one didn’t show up properly. Mark came back for the second shot, still smiling. I stood next to him, my arm on his shoulder. It was such a surreal moment.

Throughout the weekend, I crossed paths with him several times. Each time, he smiled and waved at me. Small gestures, but they meant so much.

Mark was racing with the 1931 green Invicta, number 15. I saw him during practice sessions, riding his scooter in the paddocks, always greeting me with a smile.

During one of the runs, there was a small incident — his gear lever came off in his hand. This drew attention, and people gathered around him: journalists, fans… I joined the crowd, took a few pictures, and asked for another autograph for a friend. He asked for help with the marker, and I brushed his hand slightly while helping him. Another unforgettable moment.

I told him: “I’m here just for you, Mark.”
He replied: “Oh, that’s sweet.”

Sunday morning, I woke up early for the final round. The Invicta was on the track again, and the race went well for the Knopfler / De Cadenet team.

Later, while heading back, I saw him once more driving his car — he waved at me again. One last little moment of happiness.

I hoped to see him one last time to say goodbye, but I couldn’t find him again. I didn’t want to search endlessly.

So I left, heading back home, thinking about all these incredible moments. Meeting Mark Knopfler, my guitar hero… unforgettable.

Memory notes

  • Age: 23
  • With: Alone
  • Feeling: Excited, overwhelmed and incredibly happy
  • Highlight: Meeting Mark, getting an autograph, a kiss, and a photo together
  • Strongest memory: The kindness of Mark throughout the weekend — smiling, waving, and taking the time despite everything
