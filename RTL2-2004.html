One day in October 2004

Review

During one week, the French radio RTL2 organized a contest to invite listeners to attend Mark Knopfler’s interview in their studio, on Wednesday October 13th.

The game was broadcast during the Pop Rock Station show. The presenter asked a question about Mark’s career, and we had to call in with the correct answer.

I first tried on Monday. Someone answered, asked for my answer… but I was listening from my computer, a few seconds late. I hadn’t even heard the question yet… so I couldn’t reply. “Too bad, try later.” GRRR.

Tuesday, I tried again, even installed a radio in my room to avoid the delay… but all lines were busy. Impossible.

Wednesday… same story. “All lines are busy, try later…” again and again… GRRR.

And then — a miracle. Someone answered. I finally got the question: when did Sailing to Philadelphia come out? 2000!!!

WOOOW! I won access to attend a mini concert, an interview, and meet Mark!! It wasn’t completely done yet — I still had to convince my boss to let me go to Paris… but for once, they agreed.

On Wednesday October 13th, I was incredibly excited. The journey felt so long, even if Lyon is only two hours away by TGV.

I met Colette, and we went to the RTL2 studios, 22 rue Bayard in Paris. Some fans were already waiting outside, but Mark was already inside — he had just played for another broadcast with Bernard Lavilliers (funny to see him there, he’s from my hometown).

While waiting, I met people from the French MK & Co mailing list — it was nice to finally put faces to names.

We were finally allowed into Studio 22, on the 6th floor. Small groups in the lift. Two Martin guitars were already on stage… “Mark is going to play acoustic”… that sounded promising.

We were seated on the second row, very close.

Francis Zégut arrived first, giving us instructions (no pictures… etc.). At 9pm, the Pop Rock Station broadcast started — we were live on the radio!

Then Mark was introduced and entered the studio. Standing ovation.

The interview started. I was surprised by how well he understands French, and how funny he is.

Acoustic session:

Song for Sonny Liston
Boom Like That
Back to Tupelo
Donegan’s Gone

Seeing Mark playing acoustic, that close… was simply one of the best moments of my life.

At the end, fans gathered around him for autographs, pictures, and gifts. Even if he was very busy, I managed to get his attention for a few seconds.

I gave him a photo of us from Le Mans (July 2003) to sign… and he remembered me! That was incredible.

I asked him for a kiss — and he said yes again. A spontaneous moment. I think some people around were a bit jealous.

I was completely filled with happiness.

I tried to take pictures, but unfortunately the one with Mark and me didn’t work… and there was no time to try again. They were leaving, and Mr Crockford didn’t let him stay longer. “Sigh”.

They left in a luxury Mercedes. I thought: goodbye Mark… and see you in Lyon on April 5th.

Then came the worst part of the day — going back to Lyon. I had the blues for days… but also so many memories.

And then, a few days later, I received a big email with a video attached…

I played it… OMG! It was a video of Mark and me, talking when I asked for the photo. The picture didn’t work… but I got a video instead. Even better.

Memory notes

  • Age: 23
  • With: Colette and MK fans
  • Feeling: Excited, lucky and overwhelmed
  • Highlight: Winning the contest and seeing Mark play acoustic up close
  • Strongest memory: Mark remembering me, the kiss, and discovering later that I had a video of our moment together
