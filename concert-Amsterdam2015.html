Review

Well, we had a speedway to Amsterdam with Lorraine, Lisa and Ingrid. We first dropped our luggage at the Best Western Hotel and then we went for drinks and food at the Grolsch Café before the show. That’s the tradition: drinks and food before the show.

So we walked to the Ziggo Dome, which is a very big venue. I’ve been told it has a capacity of 17,000 seats!!

We arrived quite late, so we didn’t wait too long until the beginning of the show! Once again I had a front-row seat on the right of the stage (thank you Ingrid, this is my favorite side, you know), but a little further this time! My dear sister Lorraine offered me to trade seats because she didn’t mind being a little further away, and she knows how addicted I am to the “perfect seat”!

Well, tonight’s show was rather better than Antwerp’s. The Dutch atmosphere was warmer! But still too quiet after such Parisian gigs! Mark brought back Mighty Man, and I was happy to hear this song again. It really has something special. Even if it is not my favorite on the album, I could say that hearing it live, it sounds better — it has a soul.

As you can see on the set list, Mark brought back Speedway at Nazareth! It was soooooo nice to have this song again! I was afraid that he had dropped it forever... When I saw Glen Saggers bringing the Gibson on stage, I immediately understood that Speedway was back. We all noticed that — we were on fire! The people in the front row were yelling with happiness!! What a moment, and what an interpretation tonight! It was back and powerful!

Then came the end of the show, and there I was thinking that I only had one show left and that I would have to leave most of my friends tomorrow... I was soooo happy to share this “small tour” with my sisters, Laurent and other forum and Facebook friends... Crocodile tears arrived, and the panda bear look too (my black make-up). I couldn’t control myself. The emotion of the good concert mixed together with the feeling “we’re leaving tomorrow” was just difficult.

We all went for a last drink at the pub again. Hugs and kisses time with all my friends. We wished to meet again in a couple of years if Mark continues to tour every two years...

Lisa, Lorraine and I were staying at the same hotel. We had to take a train to the airport and then the shuttle... But we missed the last train. The ticket machine didn’t work well, so I could get a tix but Lorraine and Lisa couldn’t. The thing is that I bought the ticket and couldn’t be refunded as it was the last train and it was a “today train”...

A guy from the train station told us that there was a free bus to the airport, but once inside the bus we had to pay. Then Lisa called the hotel to know where we could find the shuttle; she was told that the last shuttle was at 11:30pm... and indeed by that time it was already 12am... So we had to get a taxi.

Before leaving the airport, as there was no rush anymore, we decided to have a late snack before going to bed and stopped for excellent paninis!

My last sentence would be to thank all the people involved in making this tour possible for me this year... sharing rooms, car-pooling, Christmas presents, etc... I won't mention anybody, but people will recognize themselves ;)

Memory notes
