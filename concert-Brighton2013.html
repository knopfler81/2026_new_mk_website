Brighton, Brighton center

25th May 2013

Review

This time I had to drive to the airport quite early in the morning. St Exupéry isn't a huge airport, but Terminal 3 was quite a long walk to get to — looooong corridors!!

I travelled with the airline called EasyJet! It's a pretty nice one. I landed at Gatwick Airport, and then took a train to Brighton! I met a couple of French guys at the train station and we went together for a snack near the seashore. What fantastic weather, really! I left cloudy France for sunny UK — amazing!

Later I met up with Ingrid and Maarten; the three of us shared a room for the night. We joined Martina in a very nice restaurant before the show, some Jamie Oliver place... The food was excellent! You can definitely have good food in the UK, just choose the right place!!!

So show time arrived. Martina picked up our tix and we were actually surprised — we had nicer seats than expected!! Very front row, but not all the way to the very left as it was supposed to be!! Lucky us!! Brighton's venue is actually pretty small, though a nice place! There I also met Arthur. A pity we could only chat for a couple of minutes, but it was great meeting him again! The show started pretty early, around 7:30 / 7:45 — can’t really recall...

Like in all the performances on this tour, the guys were in a really good mood tonight! I think I’ve never seen Mark as happy on stage so far! He smiled a lot, kind of dancing! He is really pleasant to watch!

The set list was great. I missed Speedway at Nazareth, which is for me a must to be played... But I was okay anyway, I still had many other shows to attend. The stage in Brighton is pretty high; when we rushed there, I was lucky enough (or tall enough) to put my chin on the stage and enjoy the view! The sound was really good while seated, and even at the very front during the encores!

I took many pictures and I was told that Mark was watching me... I didn’t notice... What was he thinking? Well, I don’t mind — we were allowed, I didn’t use flash, and I switched off all the noisy little things and lights. When the band was about to leave the stage, Mark came over to me and shook my hand (oh gosh, he has lots of strength in his hand, believe me — that was a very firm one!!). I really felt happy with that two-second moment.

Then came Going Home with Nigel Hitchcock. I can’t explain what happened to me... Big tears arrived, it was incredible, I just couldn’t stop them. I’m too sensitive I suppose — the handshake plus Going Home was probably the main reason!

I really had a wonderful time and looked forward to the next shows — London RAH!!! We went to a nice pub for a couple of drinks, but I was a little tired so I walked back to the hotel first, which wasn’t that far. I really felt safer in Brighton than in my hometown! I truly felt on holiday there, walking back to the hotel.

Memory notes

  • Age: 32
  • With: Ingrid, Maarten, Martina and other friends
  • Feeling: Happy, emotional and on holiday
  • Highlight: Mark shaking my hand at the end of the show
  • Strongest memory: Bursting into tears during Going Home after that brief but unforgettable handshake
