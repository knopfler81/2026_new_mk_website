Golden Heart Tour

Lyon, Fourviere Arena

09 July 1996 - First concert memory

Next concert

Review

I have loved Dire Straits since an early age, and so have my parents and my brother. I can still see myself with that BIA tape in my hands saying: "Mummy, when I grow up, I will go to a Dire Straits concert".

In fact, I didn't know the name of that great guitarist that I loved so much until one day, while I was listening to the radio, they spoke about Mark Knopfler: "The voice and the guitar of Dire Straits is now on tour with a new solo album, Golden Heart".

So I immediately said to my parents that I wanted to see Mark Knopfler. �Please, please, please, I will be a good girl...� and all the usual promises. They finally accepted.

On 9 July 1996, I was 15. I stood in the Lyon arenas, waiting for the best guitarist in the world � at least to me.

We were sitting in the middle of the arena, and while waiting for Mark, a game began: everybody was making paper planes with the magazine pages handed out at the entrance. Everyone cheered when a plane landed on the stage.

But a bad incident happened. A man not far from us was holding a gun. Thankfully, he was arrested before anything worse could happen. That may be the reason why Mark delayed the show.

I really wanted to go to the front of the stage. My parents were not keen to let me go alone, but they finally agreed.

It was wonderful for both my eyes and my ears to be so close to the voice and the guitar of Dire Straits. It was my first concert. Thank you, Mark, for that wonderful moment. I am still looking for a recording, so if you know anything, please let me know.

Memory notes

  • Age: 15
  • With: My parents, and bro
  • Feeling: Excited and overwhelmed
  • Highlight: My first time seeing Mark live
  • Strongest memory: Reaching the front of the stage
