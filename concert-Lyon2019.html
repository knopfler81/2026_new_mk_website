Review

Lyon is not very far from home, so it’s always nice to be able to go back home after the show.

I spent some time walking around Lyon with Laurent and a few others before heading to the venue. Always a good moment before the concert, just enjoying the city and the calm before the music starts.

La Halle Tony Garnier is not known for having the best sound, but when you’re in the front row, it’s actually not that bad at all. Being close to the stage definitely makes a difference.

This is Mark’s last tour, so of course I want to enjoy as many shows as possible… but not as many as in previous years. I’m a bit broke (Dire Straits… yeah, I had to do it).

Still, being there, close to the stage again, sharing the moment with friends and the music… that’s what really matters.
