Down The Road Wherever

New York City, Madison Square Garden

25th September 2019

Previous concert

Review

Now let me explain the miracle that happened to me.

A few days after the concert in Nîmes, I was convinced that I had seen Mark for the last time.

Then I received a message from Vincent, offering me a ticket for the very last show of the tour in New York City, at Madison Square Garden.

I answered that I would have absolutely loved to go, but unfortunately I simply could not afford the concert, the hotel, the food and the trip.

Vincent then told me that I would only have to pay for my flight and my food — he already had the rest, and he wanted to offer it to me. He had seen me crying in Nîmes, and because his brother could no longer come with him to NYC, he had one ticket left and insisted on giving it to me. It still feels incredible.

So I said yes, and I booked my plane ticket. Sophie was also going on the trip, and we took the same flight.

We still had a few misadventures before leaving: the airline we had chosen went bankrupt. We could still leave for New York, but there was no guaranteed return flight... So we had to change airline, which increased the cost of the trip a little, but by then there was no way I could go back on my decision to go to NYC.

New York City was truly special, because I was going to attend the last show of Mark’s last tour, at Madison Square Garden — and all that from a front row seat. Yes, a royal front row.

It was also special because that day I finally met Ivan, my luthier friend from Buenos Aires, with whom I had been exchanging messages since 2003, I think. We had never met in person before, and it was such a lovely moment. It felt as if we had known each other forever — which, in a way, was true.

Bonnie Raitt was the opening act for Mark, and that was really wonderful too.

Then Mark came on stage for his final show. It was sublime, intense, and almost unreal to be able to see him one more time than I had ever expected.

Bonnie came back during the encores, but just before that we had all stood up to press ourselves against the barriers. My friend Sophie, who was seated a bit farther away in the arena, managed to come all the way to us, and we were able to enjoy those final moments together.

That concert will always feel like a gift. After Nîmes, I truly believed it was over. And yet, somehow, life gave me one last miracle.
