Get Lucky

Nimes, Arena

22nd July 2010

Previous concert Next concert

Review

After a short sleep... mmm 4 hours? I had to wake up to take my train to Nîmes! I arrived around 11am.

When I first arrived in front of it, I thought it wasn’t that big... but when I got inside for the visit, woaww, I was really impressed by how high and huge it is. It was built 2,000 years ago — can you imagine the amount of work and how long it took them to finish it!? Certainly more than a lifetime for some of them... I’m always impressed by old buildings and have a special thought for the men who worked on them. Nowadays, we would be unable to make something similar! No one would do such hard work...

Well, back to the main story! I met again the people from the AFMK for lunch near the arenas. It was very warm outside and I felt a little bad because of the heat. I was cooling myself down with some ice cubes on my back lol. Before picking up our presales tickets, I went to my hotel for a cold shower and to change into lighter clothes (it was raining when I left my hometown so... jeans and trainers were required...).

We picked up our tix at 4pm and started to queue a little hour later at the mk.com gate. The security was very strict and frisked all the bags very carefully, not for cameras but for weapons. Nine years earlier, in the same venue, during an MK gig, there was a knife fight. Mark said he doesn’t want to see that happening again.

Btw, front row again in a fully seated venue... not that bad when you’re tired from the day before! Seated venues are nice anyway: once your seat is taken, you can leave your bag, ask a friend to look after your place, and then move around a bit in the arena, go for a pee, for a drink, for a chat! I was happy to meet my French-Scandinavian friend with her little boy for a quick chat!!

The opener was the same as the day before, same feelings for me.

It was again a long wait for the band. The audience was hot again that night and we started the big wave like in a stadium. Then came the exciting last songs before they started — brrrr, shivers again! When they finally came on stage, we gave them a standing ovation! Very good mood again for the boys! Almost the same set list as the night before, with Prairie Wedding instead of Coyote. The French audience was hotter than ever — Olé Olé again indeed, they played it again, it seemed they were even waiting for it!!

Just before Done With Bonaparte, when Mark introduced Richard, we started singing Happy Birthday. He was really surprised (we had planned it with the people in the first rows).

Again, the concert went fast, and we had a little trouble during the encore... We couldn’t stay standing because of some people yelling at us to sit down; they were very nasty... So as my chair was taken by someone else, I stayed at the barrier but kneeled. That was weird, but it made Mike and Danny laugh when I waved during SFA (still kneeled...). I was very angry to finish the encore on my knees; it was really uncomfortable as the barrier was high and I’m not that tall! The end of the concert was a bit spoiled by their behaviour... but two days later it’s just an old memory. The most important thing was to be there, whatever the position. Next time I’ll try lying down... lol.

Memory notes

  • Age: 29
  • With: Friends from the AFMK
  • Feeling: Tired, hot, but thrilled
  • Highlight: Richard’s surprise when we sang Happy Birthday
  • Strongest memory: The incredible crowd energy
