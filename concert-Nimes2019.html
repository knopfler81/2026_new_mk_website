Review

Nîmes was my last concert of the tour, and also my very last Mark Knopfler concert.

Once again, we were too far from the stage for people who had been used to front rows for so many years. Fourth row may sound good to many people, but for us it felt hyper frustrating — especially for what was going to be the last MK concert of my life.

Still, by then we knew exactly how to handle the encores. We knew the precise moment when to stand up, move forward and place ourselves at the barriers so that, finally, we could be right in front of the master.

The concert was full of emotion. It was the last one for many of us, and we all knew it.

At the front, many of us were crying, holding each other in our arms, trying to enjoy and survive those final moments at the same time.

It was one of those concerts where the music and the emotion are completely mixed together, and where every song feels heavier because you know it is the end.

And then, after all that emotion, we moved on to something else... restaurant, jokes, laughter, and a lot of fun. We laughed so much with Brigitte, Kamel, Emmanuelle, Vincent, Laurent, Céline, Sophie, Bénédicte... and many others.

That is also what I want to remember: not only the tears in front of the stage, but also the warmth, the friendship, and all the laughter afterwards.
