Tour With Bob

Paris, Bercy

17th October 2011

Previous concert Next concert

Review

Very surprising, Mark Knopfler and Bob Dylan on the same tour, but for two separate shows. That’s the reason why I attended only that gig this year... I knew that Knopfler’s shows would be shorter than what we’re used to having. And I’m not a Bob Dylan fan, even if I totally respect the artist he used to be in his early years.

So I arrived at Paris Gare de Lyon in the early afternoon, went straight to my hotel, and had a quick snack. I was tired because of the trip, so I had a short nap before meeting Isaac around the venue. While waiting for Isaac, I walked a little around big Bercy and found a few “Rudïger” waiting for Mark. I preferred leaving... Too many people were here already, and I think Mark doesn’t really like that. Finally, I met up with Isaac again — it was great to see him again.

I was really disappointed with my seat assignment... Even if it was a front row, it was on the very left of the stage. I think I would have preferred a third row in the middle! Before the show, I teased Isaac about stealing his seat (which was row #1 in front of Mark). He almost wanted to switch (which I didn’t accept because it was his assignment), and then he proposed that I sit on his lap. I first thought it was a joke, but Isaac promised to behave himself, so I accepted and finally sat on the chair between his legs! It was pretty funny when the band saw us sharing the seat.

WARNING: SPOILERS AHEAD

So I can tell you that I of course loved Mark’s concert. The atmosphere for MK wasn’t that warm in big Bercy (it usually is for MK gigs); I could feel that most of the people were here for Bob Dylan’s show.

My feelings about the songs:

What It Is — I thought I was spoiled with Why Aye Man as opener... so I wasn’t.

Cleaning My Gun — I didn’t expect this one at all! A pretty good surprise, and a very, very good and powerful version.

Sailing to Philadelphia — Just lovely, as always.

Hill Farmer’s Blues — Powerful, incredible. It will always give me shivers.

Privateering — Big surprise again. I heard there were new songs played on this tour. I liked it very much!

Song for Sonny Liston — Blue Les Paul? Oh well, why not... Of course it was nice, but I think I prefer it from the older tours.

Done With Bonaparte — Not a surprise. Many people think it should be dropped... This is a song I used not to like before 2010... Now I love it (better late than never).

A Night in Summer Long Ago — For those who know my feelings for this song... No, I didn’t cry. I was surprised, but it wasn’t the same “shock” as last year. Maybe I was prepared... (He played it also in Paris in 2010.)

Marbletown & Speedway at Nazareth — Again, great performances!

So Far Away — The goodbye song... I may dare to say I’m a little tired of this one. I would have preferred another.

I’m very positive about this concert. Yes, it was short, but I knew it — I didn’t expect much! I had a great time with MK and his band...

About Bob Dylan... Well, several people warned me about the loud sound and Bob’s “bad” voice... I recall bad memories of my first Bob concerts in 2003... But I felt like staying to “give him another chance.” When the concert started, everybody rushed to the stage gates — it was a complete mess... As I was seated on the very left of big Bercy (my official seat), I couldn’t see anything... I left after 4 songs... I don’t want to say bad things about Bob; I respect the artist... but I’m sorry, I really didn’t like it.

I’m sorry, there are not many pictures of the show. As it wasn’t allowed, I didn’t bring my camera, but some friends did, so maybe with their agreement I could post some. The group photos (with the AFMK members) were taken in the basement of La Brasserie des Spectacles in front of Bercy.
