Review

After an overbooked morning at work, I finally left my office in time to catch my trains from Saint-Étienne to Paris. I arrived in Paris in the middle of the afternoon, checked in to my “luxurious” hotel (the walls were so thin that I heard my room neighbour snoring...).

Adrien from the AFMK was staying in a hotel close to mine, so we walked together to the venue. On our way, we met some folks from the AFMK and went to the brasserie for a drink (or two...) and an early dinner for me. I received a phone call from a friend who was queueing to pick up the tickets; he warned us that the organisation was a mess and that it was taking ages to get the tix...

We immediately went to the box office and yes, it was a mess, and yes, the queue was looooong... As I didn’t have to queue, I went talking with my friends Laurent, Isaac and Jeroen, who had almost finished queueing already (I think something like 50 minutes). Then a naughty idea came to me... I managed to get the two friends who had to queue to have a chat with Isaac & Co... you see?

Well, now with the tix in the pocket, we entered big Bercy. We really didn’t know what our seat numbers would be... And actually the surprise was wonderful — straight in the middle! What else??

As in London, Ruth Moody was the opening support. I don’t know why, but the more I see her, the more I like her! She’s a really pleasant young lady with great chaps in her band!

After a 30-minute break to set up the stage, MK finally arrived in a very good mood, as every night on this tour! It was also for me the night to learn how to use my new digital camera... sigh... You’ll see the pics...

The set list hadn’t changed a lot for a couple of weeks by then... I’m not complaining, I love the way it is! But a set without Sultans of Swing is kind of “a little less exciting...” That’s only my feeling, and maybe it’s the very first time I admit I’m a little disappointed, but I’ll survive! And indeed, it’s Mark’s choice and I respect that!!

As with every MK concert, we prepare — pack our stuff — to rush to the stage at the end of Telegraph Road... But in France it’s different: we are used to rushing after the song before, Speedway at Nazareth; and this year something silly happened! A guy from nowhere first rushed to the stage alone and stood in front of us! I think it took us a couple of seconds to react that it was time to follow him!

When we were at the very front of the stage, everything changed — the atmosphere, the comfort, the excitement, the heat... I started feeling bad during Telegraph Road, but I managed to focus on MK, thinking, “No Nelly, don’t fall, not now please,” and it worked!

Those wonderful shows are always too short; we wish they would never end... Till the next one... Clermont-Ferrand, with two days to wait! Anyway, after this brilliant show, I went with the folks from the AFMK for a last drink at the Brasserie des Spectacles and walked back to my hotel with Isaac as bodyguard (he was staying in a hotel close to mine too)!

Memory notes

  • Age: 32
  • With: AFMK friends
  • Feeling: Tired, excited and slightly overwhelmed
  • Highlight: Discovering our seats were right in the middle at Bercy
  • Strongest memory: The rush to the stage and trying not to faint during Telegraph Road
