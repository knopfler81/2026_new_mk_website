Review

July 14th – Saint Julien.

Always surrounded by great people. The same crew again, year after year, for what had become a special tradition.

This was the third time Mark played this festival, and the third time I was there with the same team. There was something comforting about that — familiar faces, familiar place, and that shared history around the music.

But this time, we all knew. This was one of the very last concerts. Not the last yet… but close enough to feel it.

We ran. Literally. To get to the front. And it worked — front row again.

Being there, at the barrier, made everything feel right once more. After all the ups and downs of this tour, it was exactly where we needed to be.

The atmosphere was special that night. A mix of celebration and something more fragile underneath. We were enjoying every second, but at the same time we all knew that it was coming to an end.

Every song felt a little more precious. Every moment stayed a little longer.

It was one of those concerts where nothing extraordinary happens… and yet everything feels important, simply because you know it won’t last.
