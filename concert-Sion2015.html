Review

The next day, we went to Sion with Philipp, who came to pick us up in Annemasse with his little plane — a Robin. That alone made the trip feel special.

We landed at the very same airport as the band’s jet, although we didn’t cross paths with them. We did see the jet, though, which was still a pretty exciting little detail.

My knee was still hurting from the day before, so I went again to get some treatment. I was a bit worried that I wouldn’t find my place again in the audience when I came back, but the Swiss crowd was much more respectful than the French one, and I returned to exactly the same spot.

That was such a relief, because when you’ve fought to get a good place, especially with an injured knee, you really don’t want to lose it.

The setting in Sion felt different, calmer somehow, but still very special. There was something really memorable about arriving by plane, seeing the band’s jet nearby, and then spending the evening in that atmosphere with the mountains not so far away.

Sonny Landreth was the opening act that night, which made the evening feel a little different from the previous shows. It added a special touch to the concert and made this date stand out in its own way.

Even with the pain in my knee, I was just happy to be there again, in front of the stage, ready for another night of music. That is also what I remember from this concert: not just the songs, but the effort it took to get there, and how worth it it still felt once the show began.
