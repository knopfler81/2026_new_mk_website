Review

I wanted to have the surprise of the setlist... So I've deleted my Facebook account for almost 10 days... It was pretty hard to stay away from computer, phone and other Knopfler-related things...

I don't know why, but I wanted to check how to get the USB guitar stick, to place an order...

I've opened Manchester's gig... gosh, I saw a list starting with "Broken Bones", so I realised that I was about to get more spoiled if I'd spent one more second there, so I've closed everything.

D-Day:

I've arrived in London pretty early and I met Ingrid (my usual roommate — oh no, I meant my sister!!). We had breakfast in Covent Garden, and then met Isaac for a drink. Time flies when you're in London, and soon it was time for the pre-show meeting at the Slug & Lettuce, a nice place where we had drinks and food before the show. (Actually, the venue is inside a big mall.) You'll soon notice that the tradition of going on tour to see MK is meeting for drinks, food, drinks again, the show, and then meeting again for drinks (fortunately, I don't drink alcohol...).

As it was my very first show of the Tracker tour and I didn't want to know anything about the set list, I told everyone... “Please, please do not mention the set list.” Everybody respected my wishes.

The closer showtime got, the more excited I became. I was kind of nervous... Lots of emotions, because this tour was almost impossible to make without the help of my friends and family... (my family and relatives know how crazy I am and they helped me make it happen once again).

Show time:

As usual, we got lucky with first-row tickets, and Ingrid gave me the special seat. She knows exactly where I like to sit: one or two seats to the left or right of Mark... I hate being in the very middle because of MK's microphone, which hides him, and then I can't take proper pictures...

The show started a little later than 7:30pm. The venue was very big and people arrived late, so I guess the band waited a little.

The musicians arrived on stage and started to play, then Paul Crockford arrived too (with his usual Union Jack jacket) to introduce Mark Knopfler... As I said before, I'd spoiled myself, so I knew what the opening song was: Broken Bones. Lots of people complain about the set list, but even if it doesn't change a lot from tour to tour, I don't care. I feel happy and lucky to attend gigs! Of course I wish there could be more surprises, but I take it easy! And actually I had some surprises! Yeahh, I would have never expected Broken Bones on the tour, and I really love this tune! Until the Amsterdam gig, Mark & band are touring with Ruth Moody and Nigel Hitchcock.

Here's the set list of tonight's show:

01. Broken Bones
02. Corned Beef City
03. Privateering
04. Father And Son
05. Hill Farmers Blues
06. Kingdom Of Gold (With Ruth Moody)
07. Skydiver (With Ruth Moody)
08. Laughs And Jokes And Drinks And Smokes
09. I Used To Could (With Nigel)
10. Romeo And Juliet (With Nigel)
11. Sultans Of Swing
12. Mighty Man
13. Postcards From Paraguay
14. Marbletown
15. Speedway At Nazareth
16. Telegraph Road
17. So Far Away
18. Wherever I Go (With Ruth Moody)
19. Going Home (With Nigel)

Mark looked very good, he seemed in very good shape! I was really expecting Sultans of Swing and Speedway at Nazareth to be back on the set list. At the end of the Privateering tour he dropped them, which was kind of disappointing.

Mark explained in some guitar magazines that he suffered a lot while playing Sultans of Swing, so he had to focus on playing in a different way to avoid pain... Well, it seems to work so far!

The concert length was great, a little longer than expected and than usual — maybe the luck of attending one of the first gigs of the tour...

My last sentence would be to thank all the people involved in making this tour possible for me this year... sharing rooms, car-pooling, Christmas presents, etc... I won't mention anybody, but people will recognize themselves ;)

Oh, and I hope you'll enjoy the pictures... It took me a couple of songs to set my camera, but they ain't that bad in the end!

Memory notes

  • Age: 34
  • With: Ingrid, Isaac and MK friends
  • Feeling: Excited, nervous and grateful
  • Highlight: My very first show of the Tracker tour from the front row
  • Strongest memory: The emotion before the show, trying to stay unspoilt, and finally hearing Broken Bones open the set
