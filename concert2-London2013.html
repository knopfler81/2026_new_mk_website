London, Royal Albert Hall

28th May 2013

Review

Rainy Tuesday, but I decided to wake up early and go shopping! I needed presents for my parents. It’s kind of a tradition: when I’m in London, I have got to get something for them. I first went to Camden Town for some famous and beautiful aprons for Mum, and then to Oxford Street to find a beautiful shirt for Dad (it took me ages to find the one)! It was raining cats and dogs and the streets were flooded!

Well, I went back to the hotel earlier than expected — I was soaked!! I had a little rest in a hot bath and then left for the meeting at the Stanhope Arms Pub.

There were the same fans as the day before! After a little drink and food, again we walked to the venue, and fortunately the rain had stopped, thank God!

We’re a very lucky bunch of guys, because once again we had the very front row (preorders really rock). As for this night, I was the purchaser, so we chose our tickets by drawing lots... I picked up the very same seat 21 as the day before! So what else!!

Ruth Moody opened the show again, and I enjoyed the second performance more than her first... Dunno why...

As every night, Paul Crockford introduced MK: “Gooooooood evening ladies and gentlemen, please welcome to the Royal Albert Hall, Mister... Mark... Knopfler!”

Tonight’s audience was less warm than the night before, but the show was awesome though! Sultans of Swing was skipped... (boooh) I kind of missed it, but I was still very happy with what I heard! We always expect something, and overall I’m always happy with what happens!

I was delighted to hear, for the first time on this tour, Kingdom of Gold (with Ruth Moody), and also very happy to hear the sound of this incredible blue Pensa! What a beautiful ending solo Mark played on this tune.

This tour is kind of new because every night some songs are switched! So you don’t really know what’s going to be skipped or played!

After the encore, we rushed to the stage. Oh, I forgot to mention from last night that all the seats in the RAH had been changed! So they are closer to the stage... about one meter approximately!! So no, that was not a long rush, but you still had to be ready! The guys from the back arrived very fast and surprised us!

Some stupid ones pushed us to the stage. I was actually bent over at 90°, half my body lying on the stage! Btw, this is life... I could enjoy the end of the show anyway!

Another awesome finale with Nigel Hitchcock on Going Home! What a night! What an incredible stay in England!

Back at the hotel, I had a quick snack and drink. Big hugs with all my lovely friends, and then I went to bed. I had to wake up at 5am to go to Gatwick Airport, to be at work on time at 2pm... I was so sad leaving London. I’m a very sensitive person, so again tears came... Looking forward to the next shows! 5 to go!!

Memory notes

  • Age: 32
  • With: Many friends
  • Feeling: Happy, tired and emotional
  • Highlight: Front row again at the Royal Albert Hall and hearing Kingdom of Gold live
  • Strongest memory: The final rush to the stage, Going Home with Nigel Hitchcock, and leaving London in tears the next morning
