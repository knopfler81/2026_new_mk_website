Review

Second concert in London, and actually first night at the Royal Albert Hall! As usual, we fans had a pre-meeting at the Stanhope Arms pub before the show. Traveling to see Mark is always a good excuse to also meet friends from everywhere in the world, and that's a big part of the enjoyment.

Show time:

Ruth Moody & her band were opening the act tonight. I can't say that I don't like her — some tunes are very nice, and the band is very talented though — but this is definitely not my cup of tea...

Then the “real” show started! The musicians arrived on stage and started playing some rhythm, then Paul Crockford introduced Mark Knopfler, and the show began.

Again, Mark was in a very good mood!

The atmosphere was really great tonight! There were several standing ovations! It was kind of electric this evening! Also, there was a really good interaction between Mark and his musicians. Now the band is definitely at its best, and you can see and hear it!

I got lucky because my Dutch sister, aka Ingrid, got me a front-row ticket (yes again, she's definitely the fastest)! The Royal Albert Hall is such an amazing venue. I think from everywhere in the audience you can have a good time. Btw, I would never ever trade a front-row tix for a balcony one, even with champagne & co.

The thing I like the most at the RAH is that the stage is very low, there are no security gates (even if the security guys have become “boring”...), so you can easily rush to the stage for the encores... Ruth Moody came back on stage for a couple of songs. The pity is that they had dropped Kingdom Of Gold, which was super at the O2... I really missed this duet, and even more the beautiful guitar ending solo.

Well, talking about tracks, here's the complete set list of tonight's show... (actually no surprise, same as Friday without KOG... sigh):

01. Broken Bones
02. Corned Beef City
03. Privateering
04. Father And Son
05. Hill Farmers Blues
06. Skydiver (With Ruth Moody)
07. Laughs And Jokes And Drinks And Smokes
08. Romeo And Juliet (With Nigel)
09. Sultans Of Swing
10. Mighty Man
11. Postcards From Paraguay
12. Marbletown
13. Speedway At Nazareth
14. Telegraph Road
15. So Far Away
16. Wherever I Go (With Ruth Moody)
17. Going Home (With Nigel)

Tonight the rush time was during the final solo of Telegraph Road. It's the first time in the UK that I’ve seen such a rush!!

Then came the end of the super concert, and we all went to the pub for a last drink.

My last sentence would be to thank all the people involved in making this tour possible for me this year... sharing rooms, car-pooling, Christmas presents, etc... I won't mention anybody, but people will recognize themselves ;)

Memory notes
