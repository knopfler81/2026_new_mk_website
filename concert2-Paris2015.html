Review

Third concert in London and at last the last one in the UK.

THE BEST show so far!

Once again, my Dutch sister Ingrid got me the “best seat” in the house, on the right of the stage, just in front of Richard, Guy, and Mike!

Tonight was electric. When Paul Crockford arrived on stage to introduce Mark, we all stood up, until the end of the opening song Broken Bones! Clapping our hands, dancing, woowowing (I've just invented this word...).

So here we are, second night at Le Zénith. I got almost the same seat, a little bit closer to Mark!

As we thought, the security had been reinforced, with big guys everywhere... We were even told that the production didn’t want us to stand up for the encores, which sounded a little bit crazy.

After Ruth Moody’s show, we were warned by security that at 10:30pm they would leave us doing what we wanted... I think it’s the first time I had watched my watch during a concert!!

As yesterday, when Paul Crockford arrived on stage and introduced Mark Knopfler, we all stood up and didn’t sit until the end of Broken Bones — dancing, clapping, woowoowing...

Tonight’s atmosphere was lower than yesterday... I suppose because of the heavy security, and also because the concert was not exactly the same. Mark switched a couple of songs, replacing Mighty Man with Haul Away, and So Far Away with Shangri-La... And Laughs and Jokes and Drinks and Smokes wasn’t played.

Near the end of Marbletown (not sure actually), the security guys left, and right after that we all rushed to the stage. I tried to take care of my friend Marie and make sure that she was still in the front row, because the day before someone had stolen her place at the front, and she had had to leave because she felt bad in the crowd!

Mark made a bad joke — he skipped Speedway at Nazareth! I was a little disappointed because it is one of my favourites! I just hope he won’t drop it from the set list for the rest of the tour.

Btw, this evening was not as good as last night, but it’s still wonderful to attend any Knopfler gig! We have good music and good musicians. Mark could never please every single fan, so I totally accept his choices.

My last sentence would be to thank all the people involved in making this tour possible for me this year... sharing rooms, car-pooling, Christmas presents, etc... I won't mention anybody, but people will recognize themselves ;)

Memory notes

  • Age: 34
  • With: MK friends
  • Feeling: Excited, watchful and a little frustrated
  • Highlight: Rushing to the stage again despite the reinforced security
  • Strongest memory: Watching the clock during the concert, then making sure Marie kept her place at the front when the rush finally happened
